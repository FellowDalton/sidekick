# Research: car-free routes from Harpenden to cooling day trips

Task: Research car-free routes from Harpenden to cooling day trips.

Existing wiki shortlist already had the cooling destinations: Rothamsted Park + Harpenden Leisure Centre, Batford Springs, Verulamium Splash Park + Verulamium Park, Cassiobury Park splash pools, and Hitchin Lido. This research focused on how to reach those without a car from Harpenden.

## Source facts reused / checked

- National Rail lists **Harpenden (HPD)** station at Station Road / Station Approach, Harpenden, AL5 4SP/AL5 4FQ area, managed by Thameslink, with bus/taxi/rail replacement links and live departures.
- Nominatim / OpenStreetMap placed Harpenden station at approximately **51.8148177, -0.3515572**.
- OpenStreetMap/Nominatim approximate straight-line distances from Harpenden station:
  - Harpenden station to **Harpenden Leisure Centre**: about **0.5 km straight-line**.
  - Harpenden station to **Rothamsted Park**: about **0.8 km straight-line**.
  - Harpenden station to **Batford Springs Local Nature Reserve**: about **1.2 km straight-line**.
  These are close enough for walking or cycling; real walking routes will be longer.
- The existing wiki note records: Batford Springs is a good shaded chalk-stream / wet-woodland nature option but the council says **do not swim in the water**; Rothamsted Park is the best all-round Harpenden park and links well with the leisure centre; Harpenden Leisure Centre is the closest indoor swim fallback.

## Route option 1 — least friction: walk/cycle within Harpenden

Best car-free hot-day default: **Rothamsted Park + Harpenden Leisure Centre**.

Route: walk or cycle from central Harpenden / Harpenden station to Rothamsted Park, then continue to Harpenden Leisure Centre on Leyton Road for an indoor swim if needed. The straight-line distances from Harpenden station are short: around 0.5 km to the leisure centre and 0.8 km to Rothamsted Park. This avoids train disruption, ticket faff and heat exposure from long transfers.

Alternative local shaded option: **Batford Springs**. From Harpenden station it is roughly 1.2 km straight-line northeast; use it for shade, stream-side ambience, picnic/play and nature, not swimming.

## Route option 2 — St Albans splash day: train + walk/bus

Destination: **Verulamium Splash Park / Verulamium Park, St Albans**.

Known destination facts:
- St Albans Council says Verulamium Park is an award-winning Green Flag and Green Heritage Site, **175 acres** of parkland close to the city centre, with Roman-city remains and facilities.
- Everyone Active search result for Verulamium Splash Park lists phone **01727 736080**.

Car-free route:
1. Take Thameslink / National Rail from **Harpenden (HPD)** to **St Albans City (SAC)**.
2. From St Albans City, walk through/around the city centre to Verulamium Park / splash park area, or use local buses/taxis if the heat makes the cross-town walk undesirable.
3. OpenStreetMap/Nominatim placed St Albans City at approximately 51.7504966, -0.3276687 and Verulamium Park at 51.7498710, -0.3545303; straight-line distance is about **1.9 km**, so expect roughly a 25-35 minute family walk depending on route/pace.

Working judgement: this is the best rail day-trip option from Harpenden because the train leg is short and frequent, but the last walk is the main heat risk. Go early or use a local bus/taxi for the final hop.

## Route option 3 — Watford splash-pool day: train + Abbey Line or Tube route

Destination: **Cassiobury Park splash pools, Watford**.

Known destination facts:
- Watford Council says the Cassiobury Park paddling pools are **free to all** and suitable for kids of all ages and abilities, with two splash pools, splash pads, water jets/fountains, changing facilities, toilets and a cafe kiosk.
- OpenStreetMap/Nominatim placed Cassiobury Park around 51.6597833, -0.4185116.

Car-free route A — more local / fewer London legs:
1. Take Thameslink from **Harpenden** to **St Albans City**.
2. Walk or bus across St Albans to **St Albans Abbey (SAA)**. National Rail lists St Albans Abbey at Holywell Hill, AL1 2DN, managed by London Northwestern Railway. Nominatim straight-line distance from St Albans City to St Albans Abbey is about **1.2 km**.
3. Take the Abbey Line / London Northwestern Railway service from **St Albans Abbey** to **Watford Junction (WFJ)**.
4. From Watford Junction, walk/bus/taxi to Cassiobury Park splash pools. Nominatim straight-line distance Watford Junction to Cassiobury Park is about **1.6 km**.

Car-free route B — potentially less walking at the Watford end, but via London/TfL:
1. Take Thameslink south from Harpenden to a London interchange such as West Hampstead / Farringdon / St Pancras depending on live journey planning.
2. Connect to the Metropolitan line for **Watford Underground Station**.
3. TfL identifies Watford Underground Station as **Zone 7** on the Metropolitan line. Nominatim placed the Watford tube station area about **0.3 km straight-line** from Cassiobury Park, so this is the better final approach if the Metropolitan line is running well.

Working judgement: Cassiobury has the strongest free splash-pool facilities, but it is a longer and more failure-prone car-free trip than St Albans. Check live trains before promising it to kids. If using the Abbey Line option, be aware the St Albans City-to-Abbey cross-town transfer and Watford Junction-to-park transfer are both meaningful in hot weather.

## Route option 4 — Hitchin Lido: possible but low priority without a car

Destination: **Hitchin Lido / Hitchin Swimming Centre**.

Known destination facts:
- Visit Hitchin says Hitchin Swimming Centre has a **50m heated outdoor pool open May to September**, sunbathing terrace, indoor 25m pool, indoor toddler pool and Splash Café.
- Nominatim placed Hitchin station at about 51.9531929, -0.2634505 and Hitchin Swimming Centre at about 51.9526520, -0.2814394; straight-line distance from station to swimming centre is about **1.2 km**.

Car-free route:
- There is no neat single-line rail route from Harpenden because Harpenden is on the Midland Main Line/Thameslink corridor and Hitchin is on the East Coast Main Line/Great Northern corridor.
- Plan it only with a live journey planner. Likely rail routings involve changing via London St Pancras / King’s Cross or another interchange, then walking from Hitchin station to the lido. The station-to-lido walk is manageable, but the overall trip is long for a heat-relief errand.

Working judgement: Hitchin Lido is a good outdoor-pool destination in itself, but from Harpenden car-free it should sit below the local Harpenden options, St Albans, and probably Cassiobury.

## Practical ranking for a car-free hot day

1. **Rothamsted Park + Harpenden Leisure Centre** — walk/cycle; simplest and lowest-risk.
2. **Batford Springs** — walk/cycle for shade/nature; explicitly no swimming.
3. **Verulamium Splash Park + Verulamium Park** — short train to St Albans City, then 25-35 minute walk or local bus/taxi.
4. **Cassiobury Park splash pools** — excellent free water-play facilities, but longer trip; choose Abbey Line via St Albans Abbey/Watford Junction or London/TfL via Watford Underground depending on live service.
5. **Hitchin Lido** — best outdoor-lido feel, but awkward by public transport from Harpenden; use only when live journey planning looks good.
